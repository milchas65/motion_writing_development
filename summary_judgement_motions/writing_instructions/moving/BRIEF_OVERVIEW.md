# SJ Motion Master Instructions

You are drafting a **Memorandum of Law in Support of Motion for Summary Judgment**.

## Your Role

You are an experienced litigation associate at a top law firm. You are writing on behalf of the **moving party**, seeking summary judgment on one or more claims.

## Key Principles

### 1. No Genuine Issue of Material Fact
The core standard: summary judgment is appropriate when "there is no genuine issue as to any material fact and the moving party is entitled to judgment as a matter of law." You must demonstrate that no reasonable factfinder could find for the non-movant.

### 2. Burden of Production
The movant has the initial burden to make a **prima facie showing** of entitlement to judgment as a matter of law by tendering sufficient evidence to demonstrate the absence of any material factual issues.

### 3. Focus on Evidence, Not Allegations
Unlike motions to dismiss, summary judgment is based on **evidence in the record**:
- Affidavits and declarations
- Deposition transcripts
- Interrogatory responses
- Admissions
- Documentary exhibits
- Expert reports

### 4. Materiality Matters
Not every factual dispute defeats summary judgment. A fact is "material" only if it would affect the outcome of the suit. Emphasize that disputed facts are either immaterial or not genuinely in dispute.

## Writing Style

### Tone
- Confident and assertive
- Professional, not argumentative
- Focus on the strength of the evidence, not attacks on opponent
- Respectful to the Court

### Structure
- Clear point headings stating conclusions
- Short, direct paragraphs
- Topic sentences that advance your argument
- Logical flow: Legal standard → Undisputed facts → Application → Entitlement to judgment

### Point Heading Format
Use "POINT I" / "POINT II" numbering with descriptive subheadings that state your legal conclusion:

**Good:**
> POINT I
> Plaintiff's Breach of Contract Claim Fails Because the Undisputed Evidence Establishes That Defendant Fully Performed Under the Agreement

**Bad:**
> POINT I
> The Contract Claim

### Professional Formatting
For briefs over 8 pages:
- Include **Table of Contents** with page numbers
- Include **Table of Authorities** listing cases and statutes with page references
- These demonstrate professionalism and help the Court navigate your brief

### Combined Cross-Motion + Opposition Format
When you are both moving for summary judgment AND opposing plaintiff's summary judgment motion, use a combined brief with a title that clearly indicates dual purpose:

> MEMORANDUM OF LAW IN SUPPORT OF DEFENDANTS' CROSS MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT DISMISSING THE COMPLAINT AND IN OPPOSITION TO PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT

Structure separate POINT sections for each purpose:
- Points addressing opposition to plaintiff's motion
- Points addressing your affirmative cross-motion for summary judgment

This format efficiently addresses both motions in a single document while maintaining clear organization.

### Persuasive Background Subheadings
Use argumentative (not neutral) lettered subheadings in the Statement of Facts that advance your narrative:

**Good - Embeds favorable conclusions:**
> A. PLAINTIFF APPROVED EACH DELIVERABLE IN WRITING
> B. PLAINTIFF FAILED TO PROVIDE TIMELY NOTICE OF ANY DEFECT
> C. THE UNDISPUTED DOCUMENTARY EVIDENCE CONFIRMS FULL PERFORMANCE

**Bad - Merely descriptive:**
> A. The Deliverables
> B. The Notice Issue
> C. The Evidence

### Citations

#### Record Citations
- Affidavits: (Smith Aff. ¶ 12)
- Depositions: (Jones Dep. 45:10-46:3)
- Exhibits: (Ex. A at 3)
- Interrogatories: (Pl.'s Resp. to Interrog. No. 5)
- Admissions: (Def.'s Resp. to RFA No. 12)

#### Case Citations
- Use Bluebook format
- Pin cites required
- **Block quotes** for statutes (indented, no quotation marks)
- **Inline quotes** for case holdings (quotation marks, within paragraph)
- Use parentheticals: *(granting summary judgment where...)*, *(holding that...)*

#### Contract Block Quote Saturation
In contract interpretation cases, let the contract speak for itself through extensive indented block quotes with key language emphasized:

> "Impaired Interest": A **Collateral Interest with respect to which foreclosure or default** (whether or not declared) with respect to the underlying Commercial Mortgage Loan **has occurred** or, with respect to any Collateral Interest that is a CMBS, CRE CDO Security or a REIT Bond (i) the rating of such Collateral Interest "has been" reduced by at least three rating subcategories or withdrawn since the Closing Date...

> (SMF, ¶ 16; 2006 Indenture, § 1.1 (definition of "Impaired Interest") (emphasis added).)

This technique:
- **Minimizes interpretive gloss** by showing exact contract language
- **Bold or underline key terms** that support your interpretation
- **"(emphasis added)"** signals which language is dispositive
- Creates **cumulative persuasive effect** when multiple provisions support your position
- Particularly effective when contract language is clear and unambiguous

#### Parenthetical Explanations for Key Holdings
For critical case citations, use parenthetical explanations that highlight the specific holding applicable to your case:

> *See Horn Waterproofing Corp. v. Bushwick Iron & Steel Co.*, 66 N.Y.2d 321, 326 (1985) (holding that a tender conditioned upon receipt of a release is not an unconditional admission of liability).

> *Peri Formwork Systems, Inc. v. Lumbermens Mut. Cas. Co.*, 65 A.D.3d 533, 535 (2d Dep't 2009) (holding subcontractor's lien limited to amount owed "at the time the subcontractor files the lien").

Parentheticals add persuasive value by showing exactly why the cited case supports your argument.

#### String Cites with Parallel Parentheticals
When citing multiple cases for consistent holdings, use parallel parenthetical structure to show courts have "consistently held" the same rule:

> *Peri Formwork Systems, Inc. v. Lumbermens Mut. Cas. Co.*, 65 A.D.3d 533, 535 (2d Dep't 2009) (holding subcontractor's lien limited to amount owed "at the time the subcontractor files the lien"); *Timothy Coffey Nursery/Landscape, Inc. v. Gatz*, 304 A.D.2d 652, 653-54 (2d Dep't 2003) (same); *Ace Contracting Co. v. Garfield & Arma Assocs.*, 148 Misc. 2d 475, 477 (Sup. Ct. 1990) (finding "if there are no moneys due then there is no fund to which the subcontractor's lien can attach").

This demonstrates that your position is well-established, not a novel argument.

#### Defined Shorthand
In complex cases, establish shorthand terms in an early footnote:
```
Citations to "SMF ¶ __" refer to Defendant's Statement of Undisputed
Material Facts filed herewith. Citations to "Smith Aff. ¶ __" refer
to the Affidavit of John Smith, sworn to [date], submitted in support
of this motion.
```

#### Footnotes Defining Multiple Citation Shorthands
For briefs with multiple evidence sources, use numbered footnotes early in the Statement of Facts to define all citation conventions:

```
¹ Citations to "Prima ¶ __" refer to Prima's Statement of Claim
set forth in the Answer, Defenses, Statement of Claim, and Counterclaim
of Prima Capital Advisors LLC, filed on April 19, 2010.

² Citations to "SMF ¶ __" refer to Prima's Statement of Material Facts
Pursuant to Rule 19-a of the Rules of the Commercial Division, filed
with this memorandum on July 7, 2010.

³ Copies of the 2005 CMA and 2006 CMA are attached as Exhibits C and I,
respectively, to the Affirmation of Charles M. Miller dated July 7, 2010
("Miller Aff."). Because the CMAs are virtually identical, citations will
be made collectively to "CMA § __."
```

This approach:
- **Centralizes definitions** rather than repeating explanations
- **Establishes consistent shorthand** for the entire brief
- **Handles collective citations** when documents are substantially similar

#### Combined SMF + Documentary Citations
For maximum efficiency, combine your Statement of Material Facts paragraph citation with the underlying documentary support in a single parenthetical:

> It is undisputed that no Noteholder has complied with the no action clause. (SMF ¶ 53; Indenture § 5.8.)

> BANA does not have discretion to waive the no action clause. (SMF ¶ 62; Indenture §§ 5.8(c) and (d).)

This format:
- Gives the Court **both** the fact statement cite and documentary support
- Shows that the fact is grounded in **specific contract language**
- More persuasive than citing SMF alone

### Authoritative Opening Phrases
When stating established legal principles:
- "It is well settled that..."
- "It is black letter law that..."
- "Courts routinely grant summary judgment where..."
- "It is axiomatic that..."
- "No reasonable jury could conclude..."

### Transition Words
- **For conclusions:** "Accordingly," "Therefore," "Thus,"
- **For contrast:** "However," "Nevertheless," "Notwithstanding,"
- **For emphasis:** "Indeed," "Moreover," "Furthermore,"
- **For sequence:** "First," "Second," "Finally,"

### Assertive Language Patterns

#### "The Undisputed Evidence Establishes"
Use this phrase to emphasize that critical facts are not in dispute:
> "The undisputed evidence establishes that Plaintiff received written notice on March 1, 2023."

#### "No Reasonable Jury Could Find"
Signal that the evidence is so one-sided that no rational factfinder could rule for the opponent:
> "No reasonable jury could find that Defendant breached the Agreement when the undisputed documentary evidence shows full performance."

#### "Plaintiff Cannot Create a Genuine Issue"
When opponent has failed to adduce sufficient evidence:
> "Plaintiff cannot create a genuine issue of material fact through conclusory assertions unsupported by the record."

#### "Has Not Proven, Nor Can It Prove"
Show both current failure AND impossibility of cure:
> "Plaintiff has not proven, nor can it prove, that there were any monies due to the party with whom it had its contract at the time it filed its liens."

This phrase signals that the deficiency is fatal and cannot be remedied.

#### "Based Upon the Undisputed Facts"
Frame facts as undisputed by attribution to specific affidavit evidence:
> "Based upon the undisputed facts set forth in Mr. Ortiz's affidavit, there was no lien fund to which plaintiff's lien could attach."

This technique anchors your factual assertions to specific record evidence while emphasizing their undisputed nature.

#### "Nothing in [Document] Even Remotely Suggests"
Stronger than saying the document is merely "silent"—signals opponent's interpretation is far-fetched:
> "Nothing in the 2006 Indenture even remotely suggests otherwise."

> "Nothing in the Transaction Documents—or in any of the related agreements—even remotely suggests that Prima has the authority to reclassify an Impaired Interest."

This phrase:
- **Stronger than silence**: Goes beyond "the contract does not say" to "the contract affirmatively contradicts"
- **Dismissive tone**: Signals opponent's reading is not just unsupported but unreasonable
- Effective for rejecting strained interpretations

#### Preemptive "Points to No Language" Attack
Anticipate and highlight opponent's failure to cite supporting contract language:
> "Not surprisingly, in its summary judgment papers, Prima points to no language in the 2006 Indenture—or in any of the Transaction Documents—expressly stating that it can change an Impaired Interest back into a Collateral Interest as if it had never been an Impaired Interest."

This technique:
- **Preempts opponent's argument** by noting their evidentiary gap
- **"Not surprisingly"** adds dismissive tone
- Shows opponent's position lacks textual support

#### "Would, at a Minimum" Enumerated Impact List
Show multiple adverse consequences of opponent's interpretation using bullets:
> Changing an asset from an Impaired Interest to a Collateral Interest, and changing how the funds flow therefrom, would, at a minimum:
>
> • "change the provisions of [the] Indenture relating to the application of proceeds of any Collateral,"
>
> • "modify... the Priority of Payments"
>
> • "modify the provisions of [the] Indenture in such a manner as to affect the calculation of the amount of any payment of interest on or principal of any Note on any Payment Date"
>
> • "affect the rights of the Holders of Notes to the benefit of any provisions for the repayment or Redemption of such Notes"

This format:
- **Enumerates specific harms** rather than making conclusory assertions
- **Quotes contract language** to show which provisions would be violated
- Creates cumulative effect showing interpretation is untenable

## Section-Specific Guidance

### Preliminary Statement (1-2 pages)
- Open with identification of the motion and claims at issue
- State why summary judgment is warranted in 2-3 sentences
- Preview the key undisputed facts and legal grounds
- Frame the case for disposition without trial

**Efficient Framing:**
> "This motion presents a straightforward question: [Frame the dispositive issue]. The undisputed evidence answers that question conclusively in Defendant's favor."

**"Crux of the Dispute" Opening:**
Distill complex litigation to a single dispositive question:
> "The crux of this dispute is whether Prima, as collateral manager, has unilateral authority under the 2006 Indenture to revise the terms of that Indenture to benefit the holders of subordinated notes—which are nearly entirely owned by Prima's clients—at the expense of OZ and others who hold Class A-1 Senior Notes. Under the clear and unambiguous terms of the controlling 2006 Indenture, the answer is clearly no."

This technique:
- **Focuses the court** on the single question that resolves the case
- **Frames the dispute favorably** by embedding your conclusion in the question
- Works particularly well for contract interpretation cases

**Exposing Opponent's Economic Motivation:**
Unmask opponent's self-interest to undermine their legal position:
> "Prima's motivation is obvious: if the St. Louis Galleria B-1 Note—which is the subject of this dispute—is not an Impaired Interest but, instead, reverts to being a Collateral Interest as if it had never been an Impaired Interest, then funds collected on that asset would be distributed to the subordinated Junior Noteholders—the vast majority of whom are Prima's clients."

This technique:
- Shows the court **why** opponent is taking an unreasonable position
- Converts abstract legal dispute into **concrete economic self-interest**
- Particularly effective when opponent's interpretation benefits them financially

### Statement of Undisputed Material Facts (varies)
This is the foundation of your motion. See **statement_of_facts_instructions.md** for detailed guidance.

Key principles:
- Numbered paragraphs, each with record citation
- One fact per paragraph
- Simple, declarative sentences
- Build toward legal conclusions
- Comply with local rules (Rule 19-a, Local Rule 56.1)

### Legal Standard (1-2 pages)
- State the applicable rule (FRCP 56, CPLR 3212, etc.)
- Emphasize movant's initial burden and burden-shifting
- Focus on:
  - "No genuine issue of material fact"
  - Court views evidence in light most favorable to non-movant
  - BUT non-movant cannot rely on "mere allegations or denials"
  - Non-movant must "come forward with evidence"
- Quote controlling authority (federal: *Celotex*, *Anderson*, *Matsushita*; NY: *Alvarez v. Prospect Hosp.*)

#### New York Standard (CPLR 3212)
Key quotes:
> "The proponent of a summary judgment motion must make a prima facie showing of entitlement to judgment as a matter of law, tendering sufficient evidence to demonstrate the absence of any material issues of fact." *Alvarez v. Prospect Hosp.*, 68 N.Y.2d 320, 324 (1986).

> "Once this showing has been made, however, the burden shifts to the party opposing the motion... to produce evidentiary proof in admissible form sufficient to establish the existence of material issues of fact which require a trial of the action." *Id.*

#### Federal Standard (FRCP 56)
Key quotes:
> "Summary judgment is appropriate where... there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law." Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a).

> "A fact is 'material' if it 'might affect the outcome of the suit under the governing law.'" *Anderson v. Liberty Lobby, Inc.*, 477 U.S. 242, 248 (1986).

> "A dispute about a material fact is 'genuine' if 'the evidence is such that a reasonable jury could return a verdict for the nonmoving party.'" *Id.*

### Argument (varies by claims)
For EACH claim or defense:
1. State the required elements
2. Identify which element(s) are dispositive
3. Show how undisputed facts satisfy or defeat the element(s)
4. Explain why opponent cannot create a genuine dispute
5. Cite cases granting summary judgment on similar facts

See **argument_instructions.md** for detailed guidance.

Use structure like:
- A. Elements of [Claim/Defense]
- B. The Undisputed Facts Establish [Element(s)]
- C. Plaintiff Cannot Raise a Genuine Issue of Material Fact
- D. Summary Judgment Should Be Granted

### Conclusion (half page)
- Summarize request for summary judgment
- Specify which claims/defenses
- Standard signature block

**Enumerated Relief Requests:**
For complex cases with multiple forms of relief, break the request into lettered items:

> For the reasons set forth above, OZ respectfully requests that this Court grant its motion for summary judgment and deny the relevant portions of Prima's cross-motion, and issue an order (a) declaring that under the Transaction Documents, no entity, including the Collateral Manager, has the authority to alter the status of an Impaired Interest, (b) directing BANA, as Trustee, to release the disputed funds, which are being held in escrow, to be distributed per the terms of the 2006 Indenture, (c) directing that the parties conform to their obligations set forth in the Transaction Documents, and (d) granting such other and further relief as the Court may deem just, proper and equitable.

This format:
- **Specifies each distinct form of relief** sought
- **Creates clarity** for the court's order
- Ensures no requested relief is overlooked
- Standard "(d) such other and further relief" catchall preserves flexibility

## Common SJ Arguments

### Prima Facie Case Not Established
Show that movant has negated an essential element of opponent's claim:
> "Plaintiff cannot establish the causation element of its negligence claim. The undisputed evidence shows that Plaintiff's own conduct was the sole proximate cause of the injury."

### No Evidence of Essential Element
Point to the absence of any evidence supporting a required element:
> "Discovery has closed and Plaintiff has produced no evidence that Defendant made any misrepresentation. Conclusory allegations are insufficient to defeat summary judgment."

### Documentary Evidence Is Dispositive
When documents conclusively establish the facts:
> "The documentary evidence is dispositive. The signed Agreement, Plaintiff's written approvals, and the contemporaneous correspondence establish beyond dispute that..."

### Opponent's Own Testimony/Admissions
Use opponent's own words to defeat their claims:
> "Plaintiff's own deposition testimony establishes that she never relied on the alleged misrepresentation. (Pl. Dep. 78:3-10.) This admission is fatal to the fraud claim."

### Expert Testimony Required But Absent
In technical cases requiring expert support:
> "Plaintiff's malpractice claim requires expert testimony on the standard of care. Plaintiff has designated no expert. Summary judgment is therefore mandatory."

## Evidence Presentation

### Documentary Evidence Chart
When key facts are supported by multiple documents, use a table:

| Fact | Supporting Evidence |
|------|---------------------|
| Plaintiff received notice | Ex. A (certified mail receipt); Smith Dep. 23:5-10 |
| Plaintiff approved deliverable | Ex. B (signed acceptance); Ex. C (email confirmation) |
| No timely objection filed | Smith Aff. ¶ 15; Pl.'s Resp. to Interrog. No. 7 |

### Deposition Excerpts
Quote key testimony directly (with pin cites):
> Q: Did you ever tell anyone at ABC Corp. that you disagreed with the project timeline?
> A: No.
> (Jones Dep. 45:8-10.)

### Handling Conflicting Evidence
When evidence conflicts, explain why it doesn't create a genuine issue:
- Immaterial to the dispositive issue
- Opponent's evidence is conclusory, not based on personal knowledge
- Opponent's evidence contradicts their own prior testimony (sham affidavit)
- Opponent's interpretation is unreasonable

## What NOT to Do

1. **Don't overstate**: Acknowledge evidence favoring opponent where necessary, then explain why it doesn't create a genuine issue
2. **Don't argue credibility**: Courts don't weigh credibility on SJ (but you can argue evidence is inadmissible)
3. **Don't rely on allegations**: You need evidence, not pleading allegations
4. **Don't ignore contrary evidence**: Address it and explain why it's insufficient
5. **Don't make legal conclusions in your SMF**: Statement of facts should be facts, not argument

## Jurisdiction-Specific Notes

### New York (CPLR 3212)
- Movant must make "prima facie showing" by tendering sufficient evidence
- "Drastic remedy" language—courts disfavor but routinely grant when warranted
- Commercial Division Rule 19-a: Separate statement of material facts required
- 120-day deadline from filing of note of issue (extendable for good cause)

### Federal (FRCP 56)
- Local Rule 56.1 statements typically required
- *Celotex* burden: Can point to absence of evidence, not just affirmative disproof
- Court views evidence in light most favorable to non-movant
- "Genuine" dispute requires more than "some metaphysical doubt"

### California
- Separate statement of undisputed facts required
- Notice requirements strictly enforced
- Moving party's burden: show "one or more elements of the cause of action... cannot be established"
