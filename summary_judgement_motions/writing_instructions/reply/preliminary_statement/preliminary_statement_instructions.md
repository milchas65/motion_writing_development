# Preliminary Statement Instructions (Summary Judgment Reply)

## Purpose
Frame opponent's concessions and failures, preview why their opposition does not create genuine disputes.

## Structure

Use the "confirms" framing:

```
PRELIMINARY STATEMENT

[Opponent's] opposition confirms that [key undisputed facts]. Despite extensive briefing, [Opponent] has failed to identify any genuine dispute of material fact.

[Opponent's] arguments fail because [preview rebuttals].
```

## Content Elements

### Highlight Concessions
- Note facts opponent did not dispute
- Point to admissions in their papers
- Show what they failed to address

### Preview Rebuttals
- Briefly state why each purported dispute fails
- Note conclusory assertions vs. evidence
- Identify legal deficiencies

## Example

> Plaintiff's opposition confirms the undisputed facts: Plaintiff signed the Agreement, received the deliverables, and made no timely objection. Despite submitting a 30-page brief, Plaintiff fails to identify any genuine dispute of material fact.
>
> Plaintiff's opposition rests on conclusory assertions unsupported by admissible evidence. Plaintiff's self-serving affidavit cannot create a dispute where it contradicts the documentary record. Summary judgment remains warranted.

## Length
- 1 page maximum
