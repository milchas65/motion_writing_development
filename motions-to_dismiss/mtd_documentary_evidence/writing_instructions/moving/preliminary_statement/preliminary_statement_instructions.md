# Preliminary Statement Instructions (Motion to Dismiss - Documentary Evidence)

## Purpose
Frame the dispositive documentary evidence and explain why it conclusively establishes a defense.

## Structure

```
PRELIMINARY STATEMENT

[Opening: Identify the motion and documentary evidence]

[Body: Explain how the document conclusively defeats the claim]

[Closing: Request dismissal]
```

## Content Elements

### Opening
> This motion to dismiss is based on documentary evidence that conclusively establishes Defendant's complete defense to Plaintiff's claims.

### Documentary Evidence Focus
- Identify the specific document(s)
- Quote the dispositive language
- Explain why the document is conclusive

### Legal Standard Preview
- Note that dismissal is required under CPLR 3211(a)(1)
- Reference that documentary evidence must be "unambiguous" and "essentially undeniable"

## Example

> The [Agreement/Release/Written Acknowledgment] conclusively defeats Plaintiff's claims. That document, signed by Plaintiff, expressly [releases all claims / acknowledges full payment / waives the rights at issue]. Plaintiff's complaint attempts to recover for matters that the documentary evidence establishes were resolved. Dismissal is mandatory.
