# Motion to Dismiss for Lack of Personal Jurisdiction - Case Law Effectiveness

**Purpose:** This document tracks which precedents succeed vs. fail in personal jurisdiction motions, how courts characterize leading cases, and effective case usage strategies.

**Source Cases:** Aybar v. Ford Motor Co. (37 N.Y.3d 274), Aybar III (2d Dept 2022), Appellate Briefs

---

## FOUNDATIONAL CASES

### U.S. Supreme Court

| Case | Citation | Holding | Effectiveness |
|------|----------|---------|---------------|
| **International Shoe Co. v. Washington** | 326 US 310 (1945) | Replaced Pennoyer's territorial approach with minimum contacts analysis | FOUNDATIONAL - cite for modern framework |
| **Daimler AG v. Bauman** | 571 US 117 (2014) | General jurisdiction limited to where corporation is "at home" (place of incorporation or principal place of business) | HIGHLY EFFECTIVE for defendants; limits general jurisdiction |
| **Goodyear Dunlop Tires v. Brown** | 564 US 915 (2011) | General jurisdiction exists only where corporation's affiliations are "so continuous and systematic as to render them essentially at home" | HIGHLY EFFECTIVE for defendants |
| **Ford Motor Co. v. Montana Eighth Judicial Dist.** | 141 S.Ct. 1017 (2021) | Broad specific jurisdiction where defendant "serve[d] a market" in forum state; claim need not "arise out of" but can "relate to" contacts | HIGHLY EFFECTIVE for plaintiffs on specific jurisdiction |
| **World-Wide Volkswagen Corp. v. Woodson** | 444 US 286 (1980) | "It is not unreasonable to subject [defendant] to suit in one of those States if its allegedly defective merchandise has there been the source of injury to its owner or to others" | EFFECTIVE for plaintiffs - product liability |
| **BNSF R. Co. v. Tyrrell** | 581 US ___ (2017) | Confirms Daimler's limits on general jurisdiction | SUPPORTING for defendants |
| **Pennoyer v. Neff** | 95 US 714 (1878) | Historical territorial approach - no longer controlling | HISTORICAL CONTEXT only |

### New York Court of Appeals

| Case | Citation | Holding | Effectiveness |
|------|----------|---------|---------------|
| **Aybar v. Aybar** | 37 N.Y.3d 274 (2021) | BCL registration = consent to service only, NOT consent to general jurisdiction; overturns Bagdon interpretation | LANDMARK - cite for consent-by-registration rejection |
| **Bagdon v. Philadelphia & Reading Coal** | 217 NY 432 (1916) | REINTERPRETED - only addressed effect of service on designated agent, not consent to jurisdiction | HISTORICAL - must be understood in Pennoyer context |
| **Licci v. Lebanese Can. Bank** | 20 NY3d 327 (2012) | "Articulable nexus" standard is "relatively permissive" - does NOT require causation | KEY for plaintiffs - broad 302(a)(1) interpretation |
| **Rushaid v. Pictet & Cie** | 28 NY3d 316 (2016) | CPLR 302(a)(1) inquiry is "relatively permissive" and "does not require causation" | KEY for plaintiffs - lower bar than defendants argue |
| **D & R Glob. Selections v. Bodega Olegario Falcon Pineiro** | 29 NY3d 292 (2017) | "Articulable nexus" or "substantial relationship" standard; burden shifts once minimum contacts shown | KEY for burden-shifting argument |
| **LaMarca v. Pak-Mor Mfg. Co.** | 95 NY2d 210 (2000) | Once minimum contacts shown, defendant must show "compelling case" that jurisdiction unreasonable | KEY for burden-shifting |
| **Fischbarg v. Doucet** | 9 NY3d 375 (2007) | "Jurisdiction under CPLR 302(a)(1) is proper even though the defendant never enters New York, so long as the defendant's activities here were purposeful" | SUPPORTING for plaintiffs |
| **Williams v. Beemiller, Inc.** | 33 NY3d 523 (2019) | Minimum contacts require "something more than the fortuitous circumstance that a product sold in another state later makes its way into the forum" | SUPPORTING - stream of commerce limits |
| **Keane v. Kamin** | 94 NY2d 263 (1999) | Two-component analysis: (1) service of process, (2) power/reach over party | SUPPORTING for jurisdiction framework |
| **Flick v. Stewart-Warner Corp.** | 76 NY2d 50 (1990) | Service on designated agent is "equivalent of actual service" - but addresses service, not jurisdiction | SUPPORTING - service vs. jurisdiction distinction |
| **Tauza v. Susquehanna Coal Co.** | 220 NY 259 (1917) | Addressed jurisdiction based on corporate presence/doing business, not consent | HISTORICAL CONTEXT - Cardozo |
| **McGowan v. Smith** | 52 NY2d 268 (1981) | Several visits by foreign manufacturer's representative insufficient for purposeful activities; but extensive marketing/sales activities exceed this bar | KEY - distinguishes minimal vs. substantial forum activities |
| **Codling v. Paglia** | 32 NY2d 330 (1973) | Manufacturer placing defective products into stream of commerce may be held strictly liable regardless of privity, foreseeability, or due care | SUPPORTING - strict liability for stream of commerce |
| **Paterno v. Laser Spine Inst.** | 24 NY3d 370 (2014) | Situs of injury is location of "original event" which caused injury; NY long-arm statute narrower than due process limits | KEY for defendants - situs of injury argument |
| **Raquet v. Braun** | 90 NY2d 177 (1997) | Common law indemnification requires NO actual fault by indemnitee; contribution available even if plaintiff has no direct claim against third party | KEY - indemnification vs. contribution distinction |

## APPELLATE DIVISION CASES

| Case | Citation | Holding | Effectiveness |
|------|----------|---------|---------------|
| **Aybar III** | 2d Dept (Nov. 2022) | Specific jurisdiction exists under CPLR 302(a)(1) for third-party indemnification/contribution claims; "tethered" to NY | KEY - shows specific jurisdiction CAN work |
| **Aybar v. Aybar** | 169 AD3d 137 (2d Dept 2019) | Reversed trial court on general jurisdiction; Ford/Goodyear not "at home" in NY; footnote preserved specific jurisdiction issue | KEY - general jurisdiction limits but specific jurisdiction preserved |
| **Fernandez v. DaimlerChrysler, AG** | 143 AD3d 765 (2d Dept 2016) | No nexus where defendant didn't manufacture subject vehicle or defective parts | DISTINGUISH - defendant didn't manufacture product |
| **Krajewski v. Osterlund, Inc.** | 111 AD2d 905 (2d Dept 1985) | No nexus where defendant didn't manufacture vehicle in question | DISTINGUISH - defendant didn't manufacture product |
| **Pichardo v. Zayas** | 122 AD3d 699 (2d Dept 2014) | No nexus where contract made in NY but negligence occurred in NJ | DISTINGUISH - breach occurred outside NY |
| **Singer v. Walker** | 21 AD2d 285 (1st Dept 1964) | "Place of the original event which caused the injury rather than the place of the resulting injury" controls situs | EFFECTIVE for "original injury" theory |
| **Board of Managers of Olive Park Condominium v. Maspeth Prop.** | 170 AD3d 645 (2d Dept 2019) | Common law indemnification requires vicarious liability with no actual fault by indemnitee | KEY for defendants - attack indemnification claims |
| **Dreyfus v. MPCC Corp** | 124 AD3d 830 (2d Dept 2015) | Indemnification unavailable where cause of action based on party's own negligence | SUPPORTING - indemnification limits |
| **Bivona v. Danna & Assoc.** | 123 AD3d 956 (2d Dept 2014) | Contribution available even if plaintiff has no direct right of recovery against third party | KEY - contribution survives when indemnification fails |
| **Peters v. Peters** | 162 AD3d 1378 (3d Dept 2018) | Failure to contest "essentially at home" argument in motion papers waives the objection | KEY - waiver by failure to contest |

## CONSENT-BY-REGISTRATION CASES (Post-Daimler)

### Cases Rejecting Consent-by-Registration (Majority View)

| Case | Citation | Key Quote | Notes |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| **Brown v. Lockheed Martin Corp.** | 814 F.3d 619 (2d Cir. 2016) | "If mere registration... sufficed to confer general jurisdiction by implicit consent, every corporation would be subject to general jurisdiction in every state in which it registered, and Daimler's ruling would be robbed of meaning by a back-door thief" | KEY - Second Circuit; "back-door thief" quote |
| **Spratley v. FCA US LLC** | 2017 WL 4023348 (N.D.N.Y. 2017) | "The Court agrees with the majority of district courts... after Daimler, registration to do business in New York does not amount to consent to general jurisdiction" | Federal court rejecting consent |
| **Amelius v. Grand Imperial LLC** | 57 Misc.3d 835 (Sup. Ct. 2017) | Statutes "do not adequately apprise foreign corporations that they will be subject to general jurisdiction" | State court rejecting consent |
| **Wilderness USA, Inc. v. DeAngelo Bros. LLC** | 265 F.Supp.3d 301 (W.D.N.Y. 2017) | Criticizing minority view for "brief and incomplete analytical treatment" | Detailed analysis |
| **Famular v. Whirlpool Corp.** | 2017 WL 2470844 (S.D.N.Y. 2017) | "A foreign defendant is not subject to general personal jurisdiction merely by registering to do business" | S.D.N.Y. |
| **Bonkowski v. HP Hood LLC** | 2016 WL 4536868 (E.D.N.Y. 2016) | Declining "to give New York's statutory scheme such an expansive reading" | E.D.N.Y. |
| **Chatwal Hotels v. Dollywood Co.** | 90 F.Supp.3d 97 (S.D.N.Y. 2015) | Registration insufficient for general jurisdiction | S.D.N.Y. |

**Strategic Use:** Cite these cases to establish "majority view" when opposing consent-by-registration argument.

## CASE DISTINCTION STRATEGIES

### Multi-Factor Attack Template
When distinguishing opponent's cases, attack on multiple independent grounds:
1. **Plaintiff residency differences** - Was plaintiff actually a forum resident?
2. **Purchase location** - Was product purchased in forum state?
3. **Basis of claim** - Did claim arise from forum-state conduct?
4. **Type of action** - Class action vs. individual (fundamentally different)
5. **Claims asserted** - Forum state law claims vs. other state law

Each distinction independently undermines the case's applicability.

### Vacated Case Attack
**ALWAYS Shepardize/KeyCite opponent's cases.** Finding a case was vacated (like *Schmitigal*) is devastating - opponent's authority is simply invalid.

### "Directly Enticed" Language
Use *Antonini* language: Ford "directly enticed" plaintiff through targeted advertising in forum. Applies to used product market where manufacturer promotes resale.

### "Tire Hit Its Target" Quote
From *Michelin v. De Santiago*: "The used status of the tire does not disconnect Michelin from this lawsuit... Regardless of whether a 'new' or 'used' product was involved, Michelin's tire hit its targetâ€”a Texas consumer."

**Devastating for new vs. used product distinction argument.**

## EFFECTIVE CITATION PRACTICES

### Parenthetical Explanations
Use detailed parentheticals to explain relevance:
> *Daimler*, 571 US at 128, quoting *Goodyear*, 564 US at 925 (holding that general jurisdiction exists only where corporation is "essentially at home")

### Quote Integration
Adopt vivid language from favorable precedent:
- "Tire hit its target"
- "Directly enticed"
- "Relatively permissive"
- "Articulable nexus"
- "Tethered to New York"

### Cross-Jurisdictional Pattern
Document defendant's consistent losses across multiple jurisdictions to show pattern, not isolated result.

## DAIMLER RESPONSES

When opponent relies heavily on *Daimler*:

1. **Distinguish as General Jurisdiction Case** - Daimler addressed general, not specific jurisdiction
2. **Acknowledge but Pivot** - Daimler "creates a two-sided coin" - limits general jurisdiction but doesn't eliminate specific
3. **Third-Party Action Gap** - Daimler doesn't address third-party actions
4. **"Exceptional Circumstances"** - Daimler itself noted possible exceptions
5. **Consent Separate** - Daimler doesn't eliminate consent jurisdiction (forum selection clauses, etc.)
