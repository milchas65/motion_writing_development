# Statement of Facts / Counter-Statement Instructions (Spoliation Sanctions Opposition)

## Purpose
Present facts showing good faith preservation efforts, legitimate reasons for any evidence loss, and availability of alternative evidence.

## Structure

```
STATEMENT OF FACTS
(or COUNTER-STATEMENT OF FACTS)

A. [Party's] Preservation Efforts

B. The Evidence at Issue and Its Disposition

C. Alternative Evidence Available to [Movant]

D. [Optional: Timeline Corrections]
```

## Content Elements

### A. Preservation Efforts
- When litigation hold was implemented
- Scope of preservation efforts
- Who was responsible for preservation
- What was preserved successfully

### B. Evidence Disposition
- What happened to the evidence at issue
- Legitimate reasons for any loss (technical failure, routine retention, third party control)
- Timeline of events
- What client knew and when

### C. Alternative Evidence
- What other evidence exists on the same topics
- Why alternative evidence is adequate or superior
- What movant has already received

### D. Timeline Corrections (if needed)
- Correct any misstatements in movant's papers
- Clarify disputed facts

## Key Principles

### Acknowledge What Cannot Be Disputed
- Don't deny obvious facts
- Admit what happened, explain why it happened
- Focus on innocent explanations

### Emphasize Good Faith
- Show reasonable efforts to preserve
- Explain any policies or procedures followed
- Note what was successfully preserved

### Present Alternative Evidence
- Identify substitute evidence available
- Explain why it is adequate
- Note what movant already has

## Citation Practice
- Support all facts with record citations
- Use footnotes for technical details
- Quote key documents where helpful

## Example Structure (from Charter Opposition)

```
STATEMENT OF FACTS

A. Charter's Document Retention Practices
   [Explain routine practices and why they are reasonable]

B. Charter's Response to the Complaint
   [Timeline of litigation hold and preservation efforts]

C. The Call Recordings and Transcripts
   [What happened to recordings and what transcripts exist]

D. The Scope of EarthLink's Belated Preservation Demand
   [Challenge reasonableness of demand]

E. Alternative Evidence Available to EarthLink
   [What EarthLink received and why it is sufficient]
```

## Tone
- Factual and objective
- Not defensive
- Acknowledge reasonable points from movant
- Focus on explaining, not excusing

## What NOT to Include
- Legal arguments (save for Argument section)
- Attacks on movant's motives
- Speculation or characterization
