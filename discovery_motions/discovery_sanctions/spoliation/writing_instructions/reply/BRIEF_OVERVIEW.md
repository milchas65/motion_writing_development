# Reply Brief Overview (Spoliation Sanctions Motion)

## Purpose
Reply to opponent's defenses and reinforce that spoliation sanctions are warranted.

## Your Role
You are replying on behalf of the movant seeking spoliation sanctions. Your goal is to:
- Rebut opponent's defenses
- Highlight admissions in opposition
- Address new facts or evidence
- Reinforce request for sanctions

## Key Principles

### 1. Don't Repeatâ€”Rebut
The reply should respond to opposition arguments, not repeat your opening brief.

### 2. Highlight Admissions
Point out facts opponent admitted or failed to dispute.

### 3. Expose Gaps
Show what opponent failed to address or explain.

### 4. Maintain Focus
Address only arguments opponent actually made.

## Brief Structure

```
REPLY MEMORANDUM OF LAW

[Preamble]

PRELIMINARY STATEMENT
[Frame opponent's concessions as admissions]

ARGUMENT

I. [PARTY'S] DEFENSES FAIL
   A. [Response to first defense]
   B. [Response to second defense]
   C. [Response to third defense]

II. THE REQUESTED SANCTIONS REMAIN APPROPRIATE

CONCLUSION
```

## Tone
- Confident but professional
- Not hostile or dismissive
- Focus on record evidence
- Respectful to Court

## Length
- Significantly shorter than opening brief
- Focus on rebuttal, not repetition
- No new background facts
- Example: 14 pages vs. 30 in opening
