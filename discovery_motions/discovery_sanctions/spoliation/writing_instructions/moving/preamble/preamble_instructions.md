# Preamble Instructions (Spoliation Sanctions Motion - Moving)

## Purpose
The preamble is the brief introductory paragraph that appears immediately after the caption and before the table of contents. It identifies the document and states the relief sought.

## Format

```
MEMORANDUM OF LAW IN SUPPORT OF
MOTION FOR SPOLIATION SANCTIONS

Plaintiff [NAME] respectfully submits this memorandum of law in support of its motion for spoliation sanctions against Defendant [NAME], and respectfully requests that this Court [specific sanctions sought].
```

## Content Elements

1. **Document identification**: State that this is a memorandum in support of a motion for spoliation sanctions
2. **Party identification**: Identify movant and responding party
3. **Relief sought**: Briefly state the sanctions requested

## Examples

**Standard:**
> Plaintiff EarthLink respectfully submits this memorandum of law in support of its motion for spoliation sanctions against Defendant Charter Communications, and respectfully requests that this Court impose an adverse inference instruction, preclude Charter from introducing evidence on the destroyed call recordings, and award costs and fees.

**With reference to OSC:**
> Pursuant to this Court's Order to Show Cause dated [DATE], Plaintiff respectfully submits this memorandum of law in support of its application for spoliation sanctions.

## Tone
- Formal and respectful
- Use "respectfully submits" and "respectfully requests"
- Do not argue the merits in the preamble

## Length
- 1-3 sentences
- Should fit in a single paragraph
